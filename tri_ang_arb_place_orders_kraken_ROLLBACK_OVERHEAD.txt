 Assumed Spreads for Calculation:


   * For highly liquid pairs like BTC/USDT and ETH/USDT, spreads can be as low as 0.01% - 0.05%.
   * For ETH/BTC, it might be slightly higher, perhaps 0.05% - 0.1%.


  To be conservative and illustrate the impact of the spread during rollback, I will use an average effective spread 
  of 0.05% (0.0005) for each market order during rollback. This means that when you buy at market, you pay 0.05% more
  than the mid-price, and when you sell at market, you receive 0.05% less than the mid-price. So, for a round trip
  (buy and then sell with market orders), the total spread cost would be approximately 0.1%.

  Parameters for Refined Calculation:


   * Initial Capital (C): $100 USDT
   * Taker Fee (F): 0.0026 (0.26%)
   * Effective Market Order Spread (S): 0.0005 (0.05%) per side of a market order. This is the additional cost beyond
     the taker fee when using a market order to unwind.

  Scenario: Forward Route (USDT -> BTC -> ETH -> USDT)


  Let's assume initial prices are such that 1 BTC = 30,000 USDT, 1 ETH = 2,000 USDT, 1 ETH = 0.06666 BTC (approx).

  ---

  Case 1: 1 Leg Fails (Leg 1 filled, Leg 2 & 3 fail)


  This implies Leg 1 (Buy BTC with USDT) was filled, but the subsequent legs failed. The bot needs to unwind Leg 1.


   1. Leg 1: Buy BTC with USDT (Filled)
       * Amount spent: C = $100
       * Fee paid: C * F = 100 * 0.0026 = $0.26
       * Net USDT after fee: 100 - 0.26 = $99.74
       * BTC received (approx): 99.74 / 30000 = 0.0033246 BTC (This is the value of BTC we now hold)

   2. Leg 2 & 3: Failed/Cancelled
       * No additional fees or fills.


   3. Rollback: Sell BTC for USDT (Market Order)
       * Amount of BTC to sell: 0.0033246 BTC
       * Value of BTC (approx): 0.0033246 * 30000 = $99.738
       * Fee paid (taker fee): 99.738 * F = 99.738 * 0.0026 = $0.2593
       * Spread cost (effective loss due to market order): 99.738 * S = 99.738 * 0.0005 = $0.0498
       * Total cost of rollback: 0.2593 + 0.0498 = $0.3091


  Total Overhead for 1 Leg Filled (2 Fail):
  Fee from Leg 1 fill + Cost of Rollback
  $0.26 + $0.3091 = $0.5691

  ---


  Case 2: 2 Legs Fails (Leg 1 & 2 filled, Leg 3 fails)


  This implies Leg 1 (Buy BTC with USDT) and Leg 2 (Buy ETH with BTC) were filled, but Leg 3 (Sell ETH for USDT)
  failed. The bot needs to unwind Leg 2, then Leg 1.


   1. Leg 1: Buy BTC with USDT (Filled)
       * Amount spent: C = $100
       * Fee paid: C * F = 100 * 0.0026 = $0.26
       * Net USDT after fee: 100 - 0.26 = $99.74
       * BTC received (approx): 99.74 / 30000 = 0.0033246 BTC


   2. Leg 2: Buy ETH with BTC (Filled)
       * Amount of BTC spent: 0.0033246 BTC
       * Value of BTC spent (approx): 0.0033246 * 30000 = $99.738
       * Fee paid (in BTC equivalent): 99.738 * F = 99.738 * 0.0026 = $0.2593
       * Net BTC after fee: 0.0033246 * (1 - 0.0026) = 0.0033160 BTC
       * ETH received (approx): 0.0033160 / 0.06666 = 0.04974 ETH (This is the value of ETH we now hold)

   3. Leg 3: Failed/Cancelled
       * No additional fees or fills.


   4. Rollback Leg 2: Sell ETH for BTC (Market Order)
       * Amount of ETH to sell: 0.04974 ETH
       * Value of ETH (approx): 0.04974 * 2000 = $99.48
       * Fee paid (taker fee): 99.48 * F = 99.48 * 0.0026 = $0.2586
       * Spread cost (effective loss due to market order): 99.48 * S = 99.48 * 0.0005 = $0.0497
       * BTC received from rollback (approx): 0.04974 * 0.06666 * (1 - F - S) = 0.003299 BTC


   5. Rollback Leg 1: Sell BTC for USDT (Market Order)
       * Amount of BTC to sell: 0.003299 BTC
       * Value of BTC (approx): 0.003299 * 30000 = $98.97
       * Fee paid (taker fee): 98.97 * F = 98.97 * 0.0026 = $0.2573
       * Spread cost (effective loss due to market order): 98.97 * S = 98.97 * 0.0005 = $0.0494


  Total Overhead for 2 Legs Filled (1 Fails):
  Fee from Leg 1 fill + Fee from Leg 2 fill + Cost of Rollback Leg 2 + Cost of Rollback Leg 1
  $0.26 + $0.2593 + ($0.2586 + $0.0497) + ($0.2573 + $0.0494)
  $0.26 + $0.2593 + $0.3083 + $0.3067 = $1.1343

  ---


  Summary of Refined Overhead (for $100 Capital, 0.26% Taker Fee, 0.05% effective market spread per side):


   * 0 Legs Filled (all fail): $0
   * 1 Leg Filled (2 fail): Approximately $0.57
   * 2 Legs Filled (1 fails): Approximately $1.13

  Comparison to a Successful Arbitrage:


  For a successful 3-leg arbitrage, the fees would be approximately:
  3 * C * F = 3 * 100 * 0.0026 = $0.78

  So, the additional cost (overhead) due to failure and rollback, compared to a successful trade's fees, would be:


   * 1 Leg Filled: $0.57 (failed) - $0.78 (successful) = -$0.21 (This indicates that if only one leg fills and is
     rolled back, the total fees might be less than a full successful arbitrage, because fewer transactions occurred.
     However, the intent was to profit, so this is still a loss of the expected profit.)
   * 2 Legs Filled: $1.13 (failed) - $0.78 (successful) = +$0.35 (This shows a clear additional cost due to the
     rollback market orders.)

  Key Takeaway:


  The primary overhead in a failed arbitrage with rollback comes from the market order spread incurred during the
  unwinding process. The more legs that are filled before a failure, the higher the potential overhead, as more
  market orders are needed for rollback. The code's explicit warning about paying the spread again is very accurate.


  These calculations are still approximations, as real-world prices and spreads fluctuate. However, they provide a
  much clearer picture of the potential costs involved in the rollback mechanism.
